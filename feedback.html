Stop Winging It

Why Operations Changes Everything

What if the constraint you have been fighting is actually the thing that sets you free?

This talk is being tested for paid speaking stages and eventually TEDx. I chose you specifically because you understand what it takes to get there. Your feedback shapes the final version.

Deadline: June 22, 2026

This talk reframes operations from a back-office business function into the discipline that shapes every life and every business that actually works. Using nothing more than an 18 by 14 inch suitcase and two weeks of life packed inside it, I walk through three moves that change how you make decisions, design for pressure, and build a rhythm that holds without burning you down. This is not a talk about packing. It is a talk about what happens when you stop winging it and start deciding on purpose.

The Three Moves

01

Decide What Earns Its Place

Separate the essential from the merely comfortable. Not what might be useful. What earns its place for the person you are becoming.

02

Design for the Constraint

Stop fighting the limit and start mapping it. The constraint is not a wall. It is a design brief, and the opportunity is already inside it.

03

Define the Rhythm, Not the Heroics

Heroics are a debt that compounds. Build a cadence sized to your real capacity, your real season, your real life.

The Cost of Not Deciding: The Inaction Tax

Every day you do not decide, something pays the price. Your margins. Your standards. Or the thing you said you would build when life slowed down. It never slows down. That is the tax.
